Platforms

Cvtool is written in C99 and uses POSIX features where C99 is not sufficient. The included CVL is written in pure C99. Therefore, portability is not much of a problem: any system with a C99 compiler and POSIX support will do.

The main target platforms are the free operating systems GNU/Linux and Free/Net/OpenBSD. Proprietary systems that fulfill the requirements above are automatically supported, too. Broken proprietary systems (without C99 or POSIX support) can be supported through the use of gnulib modules and the GNU autotools, but only if a user of such a system is willing to write and maintain the workarounds needed on his broken platform.

Version 0.0.1 of cvtool was tested on GNU/Linux and NetBSD systems, and to some extent also on Solaris and Windows.


Libraries

Cvtool depends on the GNU Scientific Library, version 1.6 or later. The GSL does not depend on any other software or library and is tested on many systems.
