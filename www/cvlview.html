CVLView is a viewer for arbitrary floating point data that is stored in pfs files.

All of the data processing that is necessary for visualization is done on the graphics processing unit (GPU) for optimal performance. CVLView supports range selection, gamma correction, pseudo colors, 3D views, and more.

CVLView requires the Qt toolkit, version 4.2 or newer.

Some example files:

  • tux.pfs (232K)
    An image from a time-of-flight based depth sensor.
  • vessel-skeleton.pfs (1.7M)
    The structure of vessels in the human eye, represented by the vessel midlines and their widths.
  • depthmap-q3.pgm (308K)
    A depth map from Quake3, in PGM format.
  • sar-amplitude-image.pfs (17M)
    An excerpt from a SAR amplitude image. The full image is available here. Copyright © European Space Agency (ESA). Produced from ESA remote sensing data.
  • mpi_atrium_1.pfs (8.0M)
    A high dynamic range image. Copyright © 2004 by Rafal Mantiuk. Downloaded from the HDR image gallery at the MPI and converted to PFS format with the pfstools.
